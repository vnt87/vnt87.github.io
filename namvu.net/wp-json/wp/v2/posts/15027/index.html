UCD is one of the term that gets thrown around alot recently, with businesses putting more emphasis on UX in their products. But just because it’s used a lot, doesn’t mean everybody fully understands what that is.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

User-centered design process<\/h5>\n\n\n\n

Each phase of the user-centered design process focuses on users and their needs. It\u2019s an iterative process, which means that designers go back to certain phases, again and again, to refine their designs and create the best possible product for their intended users.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

At the core of the user-centered design process is a deep empathy for the user. It\u2019s not just about what a product does for a user, it\u2019s about how the experience of interacting with the design makes the user feel<\/em>. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Here are the key steps in the user-centered design process: <\/p>\n\n\n\n

  • Understand how the user experiences the product<\/strong>. You want to know how users will engage with your design, as well as the environment or context in which they\u2019ll experience the product. Understanding this requires a lot of research, like observing users in action and conducting interviews, which we\u2019ll explore more later.<\/li>
  • Specify the user\u2019s needs<\/strong>. Based on your research, figure out which user problems are the most important to solve. <\/li>
  • Design solutions<\/strong>. Come up with lots of ideas for designs that can address the user problems you\u2019ve identified. Then, start to actually design those ideas! <\/li>
  • Evaluate the solutions you designed against the user\u2019s needs<\/strong>. Ask yourself, does the design I created solve the user\u2019s problem? To answer this question, you should test the product you designed with real people and collect feedback. <\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n
