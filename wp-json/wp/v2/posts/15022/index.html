There’s many UX Design Frameworks out there, most common among them are Design Thinking Process<\/strong> and the Five Elements<\/strong>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Design thinking process <\/h4>\n\n\n\n

Design thinking is a user-centered approach to problem-solving. It helps designers create solutions that address a real user problem and are functional and affordable. There are five phases in the design thinking process: empathize, define, ideate, prototype, and test. If these sounds familiar that’s because it is based on one of the core principles of UX design which is the Product Design Cycle<\/strong> which I’ve mentioned in an earlier post<\/a>. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Each phase of the framework answers a specific question. <\/p>\n\n\n\n
